Introduction back to ToC

Typical project management techniques are typically used in the master planning stage of construction project management. These techniques involve the representation of start and end times of activities, their duration and predecence constraints between them. Simple constraint processing techniques - such as the Critical Path Method - are used to prune the effective domains of time variables, to infer the earliest start times and latest end time of tasks. Since this plans are sometimes just represented at this level of accuracy or as this information of time windows of tasks is important for more refined planning tasks or for replanning in the change situations, the ontology should be capable of representing such information about variables and constraints.

When considering the future application of automatic plan generation in construction management, the capability to represent variables and constraints becomes even more pressing.
